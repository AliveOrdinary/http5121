V for Vendetta

Movie Review

V for Vendetta is a thrilling and thought-provoking dystopian political thriller directed by James McTeigue and written by the Wachowski brothers. Based on the graphic novel by Alan Moore, it depicts a chilling vision of the future where Britain has fallen under the strict rule of a fascist dictatorship.

The film is set in an alternative reality where a devastating virus has ravaged the world. Taking advantage of the chaos, the fascist Norsefire party has seized control of Britain, with the ruthless High Chancellor Adam Sutler as its leader. Under Norsefire's totalitarian regime, political dissidents, immigrants, Muslims, LGBTQ individuals and other "undesirables" are captured and imprisoned in camps.

Out of this dystopia emerges V, an enigmatic masked vigilante on a vendetta against the oppressive Norsefire government. After rescuing young working-class woman Evey Hammond from the secret police, he takes her under his wing and works to open her eyes to the injustices inflicted by the authoritarian regime.

Hugo Weaving delivers an incredible performance as V, imbuing the character with flair, intelligence and theatricality despite never showing his face. Over the course of the film, V demolishes landmarks symbolic of government power as he attempts to motivate and inspire the oppressed citizens to rise up against their oppressors.

Natalie Portman is also stellar as Evey, convincingly portraying her transformation from timid, vulnerable girl to courageous, defiant revolutionary. As she works more closely with V, Evey undergoes a personal journey from fear to strength as she gains political awareness.

With bold visuals, gripping action sequences and outstanding acting, V for Vendetta explores extremely relevant social and political themes that serve as a warning about the dangers of totalitarianism. It remains a thought-provoking, unforgettable film that will resonate with viewers long after watching.

Movie Information

  • Director: James McTeigue
  • Writer: Wachowski Brothers
  • Release Date: March 17, 2006
  • Running Time: 132 minutes
  • Rating: R
